Eminent Speaker Program

Name: Sridhar Chimalakonda
Profile: Sridhar Chimalakonda is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science & Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology Tirupati. Prior to this, he worked as a Visiting Faculty at IIIT Sri City during the last leg of his PhD. He did his PhD and MS by Research in Computer Science & Engineering from International Institute of Information Technology Hyderabad. His primary research interest is in the area of Software Engineering, and specifically towards empirically and qualitatively assessing quality, reuse, architecture and evolution of a broad range of software systems (such as mobile, web, games and so on).
He served as Co-Editor of several international standards (ISO/IEC 26551, 26552, 26553, 26554, 26555) in the area of software reuse and published several papers in international conferences and journals. He also volunteers for ACM SIGSOFT (Software Engineering) as Social Media Chair, Associate Editor for IEEE Software Blog, core member of ACM iSIGCSE (Computer Science Education). He is also currently serving as a Technical Advisor at IIT Tirupati, trying to promote strong programming culture, real-world and open source projects and most importantly undergrad research.

L.O.S.T (Land of Secret Treasure)

Register in teams of 2-4
Round 1: MCQ quiz; 50 questions in 40 minutes, based on C. 50% teams qualify to the next round.
Round 2: 12 riddles in 60 minutes. Questions will be related to places in the college and participants have to click photographs at that place with entire team or 2 members, as mentioned. At the end of 90 minutes, all teams have to report to the given venue and there they have to make a collage of the clicked photos and submit it to the given link within 20 minutes. no late submissions allowed. Marking criteria: Teams who have solved maximum questions correctly will win.
Tie breaker: in case of a tie, teams will be ranked according to the time at which they submitted their collage. The team submitting the collage first gets a higher rank.

#Brocode

Participants have to register in teams of 2. Both members will code 2 different problems, individually and will swap their places after every 5 minutes. At every swap, a question related to '10 year challenge' will be asked and the team answering it correctly and first will get a bonus: they will be given 40 seconds to discuss their code. Incase, two teams answer the question together, both will get 40 seconds to discuss the code. Participants have to choose one language form C/C++ before the beginning of the event and both will code in the same language throughout.
There will be 3 rounds in total. Firstly, top 15 teams will qualify to round 2 and then top 10 will qualify to round 3, where top 2 will be the winners. Scoring Criteria: For Round 1 and 2, problems will be based on pattern or series based on custom input. For the last round, coding questions will be given similar to what we see in competitive coding. Maximum time for each round is 30 minutes. At each round, the team in which both the members solve their question first and the output and code is correct and satisfactory to the mentors (people from tech. committee to check if the code is acceptable) will move to the next round. Next preference will be given to the team in which only one member was able to solve the problem correctly. Incase of tie, a set of tie breaker questions, based on C/C++ will be asked. Incase no team solves any question in 30 mins, the time for that round will be extended by 10 minutes.
